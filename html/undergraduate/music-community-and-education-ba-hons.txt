<section class="overview" id="overview">

            <div >


                <h2 class="heading-2 -bold -plum-text">
                    Course overview <span class="testUrl"></span>
                </h2>

                <div class="visible-content">
                    <p><strong>Pursue your creative endeavours &ndash; whether that&rsquo;s as a peripatetic music teacher, a classroom music specialist, an animateur, or an arts practitioner working with diverse communities.</strong></p>
<p>To thrive in today&rsquo;s fast-moving music culture, you&rsquo;ll need a wide range of musical and technical skills, and to be able to work confidently with others across a range of professional contexts.</p>
<p>Your practical, applied work will culminate in a large-scale project that will be a stepping stone into your chosen career.</p>
<p>You'll study a range of technical skills to enable you to realise your best work. Core skills might include: curriculum design, project management, music theory and analysis, pedagogical theory, fluency with key pieces of music software, research skills, and festival programming.</p>
<p>You'll receive robust training in the critical and contextual dimensions of the study of music, working with expert staff to explore, interrogate and challenge received wisdom about the value, status and significance of music in our society.</p>
<p><strong>BA Music (Community and Education)</strong>&nbsp;is a variant of the <a href="https://www.hull.ac.uk/study/undergraduate/music-ba-hons">BA Music</a> programme. This means that you will share the same modules as other BA Music students, although when there is optionality in each module, your assessments, and specialist cluster groups, will focus on Community and Education.</p>
<p>Creativity lives and breathes at the University of Hull. Always has done, always will. It&rsquo;s central to what universities do. But at Hull, we&rsquo;re building something that goes far beyond our four walls.&nbsp;</p>
                </div>

                                  <h3>First year modules</h3>



                                
                                  <div class="modules-container year-one-mods">

    <div class="frame" id="frameYOne">
      <ul>
                <li class="items">
                  <p class="module-heading"><strong>Core modules</strong></p>

                      <div>
                          <h3>Creating Recorded Music</h3>   
                          <p>Writer? Performer? Producer? Whatever your focus, you&rsquo;ll work with fellow musicians to fully realise a musical recording. You&rsquo;ll learn how to collaborate with others, each with different skills and roles, to create original music (including songwriting, instrumental works, electronic tracks and anything in-between) or to capture performance as a recorded artefact. You&rsquo;ll finish the module with a portfolio-ready work.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>Creating Live Music</h3>   
                          <p>Channel your creativity into a live music event to develop your technical and artistic skills whatever your musical interests. You&rsquo;ll collaborate with music and music production students to create a unique and original live music event, alongside developing core skills in project management, repertoire development and artistic presentation.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>Core Music Techniques</h3>   
                          <p>How does music work? How is it put together? Providing you with an essential proficiency in music theory and enabling you to realise your creative projects, you will develop your understanding of notation, harmony, melody, rhythm, alongside listening skills. You will form a portfolio for your final assessment from tasks across a variety of musical styles and techniques.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>Applied Music Skills</h3>   
                          <p>Get an in-depth understanding of how music is put together, along with how and why harmonic provides such a powerful force in music&rsquo;s structure. Based on a range of musical styles, you&rsquo;ll be given the tools and techniques to analyse structural and harmonic perspectives, and develop crucial listening skills for practical musicianship.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>Music, Criticism and Culture</h3>   
                          <p>Music can be profoundly significant for individuals in their daily lives, for politically-charged movements, and for entire cultures. This module will teach you different ways to ask such questions of music, and will help you to apply ideas derived from core literature to contemporary case studies.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>Music and its Histories</h3>   
                          <p>This introduces you to narratives that characterise histories of music across different historical periods, technologies, and styles. You will explore these while also pursuing specialist studies relevant to your degree pathway. Through a range of different music, you will look at their histories and the assumptions behind the construction of such histories.</p>
                      </div>
                </li>
      </ul>
    </div>

                            <div id="year-two-panel" class="panel year-two">
                            
                                
                                  <h3>Second year modules</h3>


                                
                                  <div class="modules-container year-two-mods">

    <div class="frame" id="frameYTwo">
      <ul>
                <li class="items">
                  <p class="module-heading"><strong>Core modules</strong></p>

                      <div>
                          <h3>Individual Creative Project</h3>   
                          <p>What would you like to create? This module gives you the time, space, and expert support that you need to bring your individual project to life. You&rsquo;ll be supported through planning, executing, and completion &ndash; which may be a performance, composition, recording, songwriting EP, educational practical project, or piece of sound design.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>Collaborative Creative Project</h3>   
                          <p>Creative project management will explore the key methods used in a broad range of creative industries to organise and manage complex projects.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>Specialist Music Skills</h3>   
                          <p>We can study music in a multitude of ways. Designed to equip you with core music research skills and specialist techniques useful in a variety of applications, this module will help you to feel confident about gathering, handling and evaluating information. At the same time, you&rsquo;ll hone your practical skills through focused study.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>Music and Its Objects</h3>   
                          <p>Explore how music has been shaped by the objects we associate with it: instruments, technologies, sound recordings, and notations. You will develop a critical approach to thinking about these, helping you to deepen your understanding of the historical context and critical significance of your own music-making activities.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>Case Studies in Contemporary Practice</h3>   
                          <p>Music is a diverse field that practitioners explore in many ways &ndash; such as performances, compositions, installations, community projects, music therapy, industry-related practices, and music psychology. This module interrogates the concepts, methods, philosophies, and motivations that underpin them. You will also benefit from sessions with leading practitioners as you prepare your own critique of a chosen case study.</p>
                      </div>
                </li>
                <li class="items">
                  <p class="module-heading"><strong>Optional modules</strong></p>

                      <div>
                          <h3>Professional Music Skills</h3>   
                          <p>How should you prepare for your music career? You will explore roles, discover what professionals really do, and find out how to maximise your future employment opportunities in a vibrant and growing sector. It will give you an in-depth understanding of your chosen field, as well as set you up for your next steps as a professional.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>Music and Sound for Media</h3>   
                          <p>This practical project-based module supports the development of specialised creative and technical skills involved in the production of sound and music for visual media. You can undertake a variety of projects, including films, games, music videos, interactive installations, and performances with visual projection. As projects are developed, general principles relating to the interaction of sight and sound will be explored, such as functionality, audio-visual correspondence and implementation.</p>
                      </div>
                </li>
      </ul>
    </div>

                                  <h3>Final year modules</h3>


                                
                                  <div class="modules-container year-three-mods">

    <div class="frame" id="frameYThree">
      <ul>
                <li class="items">
                  <p class="module-heading"><strong>Core modules</strong></p>

                      <div>
                          <h3>Major Project (Music)</h3>   
                          <p>What would you like to create? You will carry out a substantial piece of work in your chosen area. Whether that&rsquo;s large-scale live performance, an original album, a critical dissertation, a portfolio of compositions, music for film or game, or a cutting-edge research project, we&rsquo;ll give you the support, facilities and expertise you need, along with some key planning and management skills.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>Working in Music</h3>   
                          <p>Gain essential insight into your future career by taking part in a placement. Whether you&rsquo;re going into performing on stage or in the studio, working in broadcast or live events, or going into teaching, we&rsquo;ll set up a placement so that you can start learning the ropes of your chosen career during your degree.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>Questioning Music</h3>   
                          <p>Imagine that what you&rsquo;ve always thought was true about music actually wasn&rsquo;t. What if there were questions about music that you hadn&rsquo;t even thought could be asked? Challenging your assumptions about music and its value, which might lead you to think differently, the skills you learn will be highly transferable and prepare you for a range of career paths.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>The Future of the Arts</h3>   
                          <p>What is the environmental cost of a single Spotify stream, an online gaming session, or of going to a gig, concert or to see a play? How much carbon does that YouTube movie trailer release? Our ability to engage with arts and culture has been made almost effortless, but at what environmental cost? By reflecting on your own creative practices, you&rsquo;ll be equipped with the skills and understanding you&rsquo;ll need to shape the arts practices of the future.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>Generating Insights into Music</h3>   
                          <p>Music changes in the blink of an eye &ndash; what it is, how it is made, and how we listen to it. This module will enable you to pose probing questions to generate new insights into music. You&rsquo;ll define your topic and explore this in detail before sharing your insights &ndash; giving you crucial skills and sharpening your abilities to articulate your ideas.</p>
                      </div>
                </li>
      </ul>
    </div>
