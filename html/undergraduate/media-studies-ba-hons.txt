                                  <h3>First year modules</h3>



                                
                                  <div class="modules-container year-one-mods">

    <div class="frame" id="frameYOne">
      <ul>
                <li class="items">
                  <p class="module-heading"><strong>Compulsory</strong></p>

                      <div>
                          <h3>Film Form</h3>   
                          <p>Learn the fundamental elements of film style and structure. Through analysing a range of recent popular films, you will understand how mise-en-sc&egrave;ne, cinematography, editing and sound function within film style. You will also learn to analyse the narrative structures of successful popular films. The ways of seeing you acquire on this module will make you a better film viewer, and a better filmmaker.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>History of Hollywood Cinema</h3>   
                          <p>In this module, you will learn about the History of Hollywood Cinema, looking at its industrial, technological, aesthetic, and genre histories. It serves as a foundational module for the Film Studies course, so the information you learn will carry you through your degree. You will also learn how to read/understand/apply a variety of academic sources to the study of your subject, a skill that will be necessary throughout your course.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>Film Analysis and Criticism</h3>   
                          <p>This module will make you a better film viewer, and a better film critic. You will experience how detailed attention and focused critical discussion reveal the achievements and possibilities of film art. Each week, you will be introduced to a case study film, watch it in full, read critical writing, and discuss the film in detail in small group seminars. You will write a series of short pieces of critical writing, which will prepare you for the module&rsquo;s assessments: a close analysis of a short film sequence, and an essay evaluating one of the module&rsquo;s films.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>Media Methods</h3>   
                          <p>This module will familiarise you with Media Studies as an academic discipline and a set of tools for producing new knowledge and information. You will gain valuable insight into Media Studies as a complex and developing discipline, which encompasses approaches drawn from academic fields such as literary studies, film studies, sociology, business and economics.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>Screen, Nation, Identity</h3>   
                          <p>Discover different national cinemas and examine the relationship between a country&rsquo;s sense of history and identity, and the stories that it tells about itself on screen.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>Introduction to Filmmaking</h3>   
                          <p>Get hands-on with filmmaking equipment in our studio and editing facilities, and on location, completing a series of group tasks that equip you with basic practical skills you can build on throughout the rest of your degree.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>Introduction to Television Studies</h3>   
                          <p>You'll be introduced to the study of television as a medium and the discipline of Television Studies. The module will examine the ways in which television has been conceptualised, theorised, and analysed by television and media scholars.</p>
                      </div>
                </li>
      </ul>
    </div>

                                  <h3>Second year modules</h3>


                                
                                  <div class="modules-container year-two-mods">

    <div class="frame" id="frameYTwo">
      <ul>
                <li class="items">
                  <p class="module-heading"><strong>Compulsory</strong></p>

                      <div>
                          <h3>Representing Reality</h3>   
                          <p>One of the key roles of our media is to represent the world, its happenings, and its diversity to us as accessibly and truthfully as possible. How do different forms of media &ndash; documentaries, reality television, soap opera, news reporting, drama-documentaries, radio, fiction films &ndash; go about this task? Why do media texts and media companies so often fail in their duty to represent reality? Through conceptual reflection and case study exploration, this module seeks to explore these questions.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>Television and Factual Production</h3>   
                          <p>In this module, you will learn the skills of factual screen production. Working in small groups, you will create short items of broadcast quality to form part of a magazine-style programme.</p>
                      </div>
                </li>
                <li class="items">
                  <p class="module-heading"><strong>Optional</strong></p>

                      <div>
                          <h3>Screening Genders</h3>   
                          <p>Critically examine the representation of gender on screen through the lens of feminist film theory.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>Creative Moving Image Practices</h3>   
                          <p>Explore the creative possibilities of moving image media. Leave storytelling behind, and develop a concept for a short moving image piece, which will then become your own independent production.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>Creative Business: Pitch and Design</h3>   
                          <p>Develop your understanding of the tools and techniques used to generate a business idea in the pre-production stage, and create a pitch deck for a product of your own design.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>Work-Based Learning (Digital Media)</h3>   
                          <p>Develop your CV by undertaking a work placement as part of your course.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>American Alternative Cinema</h3>   
                          <p>Explore and apply theoretical approaches to aesthetic forms and themes, modes of production, and audience and media reception of categories such as &lsquo;underground cinema&rsquo; and &lsquo;indiewood&rsquo;. Develop your critical and theoretical faculties so that you can actively engage and participate in debates that encompass both mainstream and alternative American filmmaking practices.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>3D Asset Design</h3>   
                          <p>Gain a grounding in the fundamentals of 3D Design. Learn the basics of modelling, textures, 3D painting and rendering, and design your own 3D animation and portfolio.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>Visual Design </h3>   
                          <p>You'll develop graphic solutions for a range of platforms. In addition, you'll continue to explore design research with a view to developing more advanced solutions.</p>
                      </div>
                </li>
      </ul>

                                  <div class="modules-container year-three-mods">

                      <div>
                          <h3>Dissertation</h3>   
                          <p>You will make an original contribution to research by designing, carrying out and writing up your own project on a topic you choose, supported by your dissertation supervisor.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>Screen Production Project 1: Planning and Pre-Production</h3>   
                          <p>Turn your film idea into a workable and professional project before you step into production. Learn about what it takes to create a film before pitching your idea, ready for the next steps.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>Screen Production Project 2: Filming and Post-Production</h3>   
                          <p>Create your film. Take the reins and follow the project through from pre-production to shooting, editing, screening and review.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>Is Television History?</h3>   
                          <p>Reflect on the ways in which television can both <em>do</em> history and <em>be</em> history, with case studies ranging from period dramas to historical documentaries.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>Disney Studies</h3>   
                          <p>An in-depth exploration of the history and impact of Disney's global entertainment empire.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>Telling the Truth: American Documentary Culture from the Muckrakers to Fake News</h3>   
                          <p>Explore how photographers and filmmakers have documented American society from the early 20th century to the present day, on a module with a focus on visual culture.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>East Asian Cinema</h3>   
                          <p>Examine the dynamic and diverse film output of countries including China, South Korea and Japan, and reflect on film as a 'transnational' phenomenon.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>Global Nightmares: Contemporary Horror Cinema From Around the World</h3>   
                          <p>Approach the horror film from the larger context of world cinema in order to assess if America&rsquo;s &lsquo;collective nightmare&rsquo; is indeed part of a larger trend that feeds into the concept of &lsquo;global nightmares&rsquo;. In particular, the module will focus on how world horror cinema represents issues of the family, gender, and the &lsquo;Other&rsquo; alongside more contemporary concerns such as pandemic disease, environmental collapse, immigration, and terrorism.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>3D Character Design</h3>   
                          <p>Apply principles of animation to bring life to your 3D characters. Learn and apply different workflows and explore advanced features of 3D animation.</p>
                      </div>
                </li>
      </ul>

        How you'll study
