            <div >


                <h2 class="heading-2 -bold -plum-text">
                    Course overview <span class="testUrl"></span>
                </h2>

                <div class="visible-content">
                    <p><strong>Build the creative, organisational, personal and technical skills to thrive in the creative and media industries, where you&rsquo;ll tell the stories of the region, the nation and the world in all their diversity.</strong></p>
<p>You&rsquo;ll benefit from the Screen Yorkshire Connected Campus scheme, which connects regional universities to the booming regional screen industry with careers advice, skills workshops, and developing collaboration between universities and regional film and television producers.</p>
<p>Develop your skills and experience across a range of production spaces, from a multi-purpose studio to a fully operational on-campus theatre, and gain experience in location filmmaking. And you&rsquo;ll have exclusive access to our <a href="/faculties/subjects/features/school-of-arts-facilities" target="_blank" rel="noopener">media labs</a>, home to the latest digital media software and hardware used in the industry today.</p>
<p>These skills are developed through two main programme strands. The first focuses on the creative processes of media production, including technical expertise, individual and group planning, and safe working practices. The second focuses on key aspects of the sector, including diversity, entrepreneurship, and the legal and organisational realities of the sector, which are crucial for any practicing professional to understand and embrace.</p>
<p>Creativity lives and breathes at the University of Hull. Always has done, always will. It&rsquo;s central to what universities do. But at Hull, we&rsquo;re building something that goes far beyond our four walls.&nbsp;</p>

                                  <h3>First year modules</h3>
        <p><p>You&rsquo;ll study specialist modules in media production techniques for location and studio, including cinematography, lighting, sound recording, production management and health and safety, and gain post-production skills in audio and editing.</p></p>



                                
                                  <div class="modules-container year-one-mods">

    <div class="frame" id="frameYOne">
      <ul>
                <li class="items">
                  <p class="module-heading"><strong>Core modules</strong></p>

                      <div>
                          <h3>Film Form</h3>   
                          <p>Learn the fundamental elements of film style and structure. Through analysing a range of recent popular films, you will understand how mise-en-sc&egrave;ne, cinematography, editing and sound function within film style. You will also learn to analyse the narrative structures of successful popular films. The ways of seeing you acquire on this module will make you a better film viewer, and a better filmmaker.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>Introduction to Filmmaking</h3>   
                          <p>Get hands-on with filmmaking equipment in our studio and editing facilities, and on location, completing a series of group tasks that equip you with basic practical skills you can build on throughout the rest of your degree.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>History of Hollywood Cinema</h3>   
                          <p>In this module, you will learn about the History of Hollywood Cinema, looking at its industrial, technological, aesthetic, and genre histories. It serves as a foundational module for the Film Studies course, so the information you learn will carry you through your degree. You will also learn how to read/understand/apply a variety of academic sources to the study of your subject, a skill that will be necessary throughout your course.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>Introduction to Documentary Filmmaking</h3>   
                          <p>Documentaries are windows to the wider world. You'll explore the rich and varied world of documentary films - from personal narratives to large productions that take stock of global movements. You will develop filmmaking skills in this intense practice-based course, and creatively and innovatively engage with the art of documentary filmmaking as you conceptualise and complete your own short documentary.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>Media Methods</h3>   
                          <p>This module will familiarise you with Media Studies as an academic discipline and a set of tools for producing new knowledge and information. You will gain valuable insight into Media Studies as a complex and developing discipline, which encompasses approaches drawn from academic fields such as literary studies, film studies, sociology, business and economics.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>Exploring Post-production Practices</h3>   
                          <p>Develop your understanding of editing and post-production through a series of hands-on exercises.</p>
                      </div>
                </li>
      </ul>
    </div>

                                  <h3>Second year modules</h3>


                                
                                  <div class="modules-container year-two-mods">

    <div class="frame" id="frameYTwo">
      <ul>
                <li class="items">
                  <p class="module-heading"><strong>Core modules</strong></p>

                      <div>
                          <h3>Intermediate Techniques (Creative Industries)</h3>   
                          <p>Develop skills in areas including scenography, stage management, scriptwriting, performance, and movement.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>Representing Reality</h3>   
                          <p>One of the key roles of our media is to represent the world, its happenings, and its diversity to us as accessibly and truthfully as possible. How do different forms of media &ndash; documentaries, reality television, soap opera, news reporting, drama-documentaries, radio, fiction films &ndash; go about this task? Why do media texts and media companies so often fail in their duty to represent reality? Through conceptual reflection and case study exploration, this module seeks to explore these questions.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>Television and Factual Production</h3>   
                          <p>In this module, you will learn the skills of factual screen production. Working in small groups, you will create short items of broadcast quality to form part of a magazine-style programme.</p>
                      </div>
                </li>
                <li class="items">
                  <p class="module-heading"><strong>Optional modules</strong></p>

                      <div>
                          <h3>From the alphabet to the internet: a history of communication</h3>   
                          <p>Using historical case studies in combination with theoretical models, you will explore questions including i) What aspects of economics and culture can promote or inhibit the development and dissemination of media technologies? ii) What factors determine the uses to which new technologies of communication are put? iii) Does cultural change drive technological change, or vice versa, or both?</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>Screening Genders</h3>   
                          <p>Critically examine the representation of gender on screen through the lens of feminist film theory.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>Music and Sound for Media</h3>   
                          <p>This practical project-based module supports the development of specialised creative and technical skills involved in the production of sound and music for visual media. You can undertake a variety of projects, including films, games, music videos, interactive installations, and performances with visual projection. As projects are developed, general principles relating to the interaction of sight and sound will be explored, such as functionality, audio-visual correspondence and implementation.</p>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                          <h3>Collaborative Creative Project</h3>   
                          <p>Creative project management will explore the key methods used in a broad range of creative industries to organise and manage complex projects.</p>
                      </div>
                </li>
      </ul>
    </div>
